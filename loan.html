✅ Basic Requirements to Qualify for a Loan

  1. Valid Identification

    National ID, passport, or driver's license.

    Must prove you're of legal age (typically 18+).

  2. Proof of Income or Revenue

    Recent pay slips (for individuals) OR

    Bank statements, business financials, or grant receipts (for business/project loans).

  3. Credit History / Credit Score

    Lenders may check your repayment history.

    For new businesses or youth groups, lenders may look at the team’s financial behavior or community credibility.

  4. Business or Project Plan (if applicable)

    Detailed plan showing:

    What the vocational establishment will do.

    Estimated costs and revenue.

    Social or community impact.

    Timeline for repayment.

  5. Collateral or Guarantor (optional, but often required)

    An asset (e.g., land, equipment) or a person who will guarantee the loan.

  6. Bank Account

    Most lenders will require an active bank account to deposit funds and track repayments.

  7. Loan Purpose Statement

    A brief explanation of what the loan is for (must be legal and clearly stated).

  8. Good Standing with the Community (especially for youth groups)

    Letters of recommendation or endorsements from local leaders or organizations.

  9. Group/Team Documents (for collective efforts like your vocational project)

    Registered name or constitution of the group.

    List of members and roles.

    Meeting minutes showing the agreement to apply for a loan.
